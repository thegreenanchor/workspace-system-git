# Writing Natural Punctuation Standard

## Scope
Use this standard for blogs, articles, landing-page copy, emails, newsletters, captions, scripts, and any long-form or short-form marketing writing produced in this workspace.

## Rule
Avoid em dashes in body copy by default.

## Why
Frequent em-dash usage is a recurring AI tell in generated writing. It makes otherwise solid copy feel synthetic, patterned, and over-smoothed.

## Minimum enforcement
- Prefer periods, commas, parentheses, or a colon instead of an em dash.
- Use em dashes only when they are clearly the best editorial choice.
- Never stack or repeat em dashes for emphasis.
- If a draft contains multiple em dashes, revise them out unless there is a specific editorial reason to keep them.

## Preferred replacements
- Use a period to split thoughts cleanly.
- Use a comma when the sentence should stay continuous.
- Use a colon when introducing an explanation, example, or list.
- Use parentheses for short side notes.

## Examples
- Instead of `This matters - especially for founders.` use `This matters, especially for founders.`
- Instead of `There is one issue - visibility.` use `There is one issue: visibility.`
- Instead of `The system - when used correctly - compounds.` use `The system, when used correctly, compounds.`
